Industry Color Analysis
Light vs. Dark in the TAMP World
You're absolutely right — the overwhelming majority of TAMP and financial advisor platforms use light themes. Here's the breakdown and what it means for your choice.

The Industry Landscape

Almost every major TAMP uses a predominantly white/light background: Orion, AssetMark, GeoWealth, SEI, Envestnet, Brookstone — they all run light themes with blue or green accents. The reasoning is practical: advisors spend hours in these portals reviewing data tables, account lists, and reports. Light backgrounds reduce eye strain for prolonged use and make dense tabular data more scannable. Black Diamond is one of the few that leans darker, and even they use a mostly light dashboard with dark nav elements.

What This Means for Triumph Managed Program

A light theme is the safer, more conventional choice — and probably the right default for daily-use advisor portal. But here's the play: use a dark theme for marketing and presentation contexts (login page, model factsheets, pitch materials) and a light theme for the working portal (dashboard, account management, reports). This gives you the premium feel of dark for first impressions while keeping the working environment comfortable. Below, I've mocked up both.

About the Flourish

The decorative scroll from your document logo (the S-curve bracket under "Private Wealth Management") is adapted here as a lighter, more refined flourish that frames the "MANAGED PROGRAM" text. Recommendation
The Dual-Theme Strategy

Go with light theme as the default working environment — it's what every major TAMP does, and for good reason. Advisors will spend hours in this portal reviewing accounts and model data. The warm cream (#F8F5F0) instead of stark white differentiates you from the Orions and AssetMarks of the world while keeping readability high.

Use the dark theme for high-impact moments: the login screen, model factsheet PDFs, pitch decks, and onboarding flows. This is where the full flourish treatment and shield mark look their best and make the strongest impression.

The decorative flourish from your document logo works beautifully as a subtle separator element. Use it on the stacked logo, login page, and formal documents — but keep it off the working dashboard where clean simplicity wins. The navy nav bar on the light portal carries enough brand presence without needing the flourish in everyday UI.
