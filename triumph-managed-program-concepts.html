Design Rationale

This concept takes the existing Triumph brand DNA (navy, gold, serif typography, shield imagery) and refines it into a more exclusive, institutional feel. The shield + T monogram creates a crest-like mark that nods to the parent "Triumph" name without replicating the full logo. Bronze replaces bright gold for a more understated, modern luxury feel. Advisors immediately recognize the connection to Triumph but understand this is a distinct, professional-tier platform.

Design Rationale

This direction breaks further from the parent brand to position the Managed Program as a modern, technology-forward platform. The abstract peak/arrow symbol represents growth and upward trajectory — core to what a model program delivers. The all-sans-serif type system (Sora) creates a clean, dashboard-native aesthetic that feels purpose-built for advisors interacting with data. The teal accent keeps it in the "finance blue" family without being identical to the parent brand.

Design Rationale

This concept uses the "TMP" monogram as the anchor — a clean abbreviation that advisors will naturally adopt. The laurel motif (on the stacked version) subtly references the "Triumph" name and its classical victory associations. Sage green is a departure from the parent brand's blue/gold but maintains a financial-services sensibility while feeling fresh and distinct. The gold thread accent provides just enough visual DNA to connect it back to Triumph Capital Management.

Summary

Choosing the Right Direction

Concept 1 (Heritage Elevated) is the safest bet if you want advisors to immediately feel "this is Triumph." The shield + bronze palette creates a premium sub-brand that lives comfortably next to the main site. Best for a traditional advisor audience.

Concept 2 (Modern Authority) is the pick if you want the portal to feel like a modern platform first and foremost — think Black Diamond, Orion, or Riskalyze in terms of UI energy. The teal + charcoal palette signals tech sophistication. Best if the portal will be data-heavy with analytics features.

Concept 3 (Refined Distinction) splits the difference — it's clearly related to Triumph through the gold thread and serif typography, but the sage green gives it a unique personality. The TMP monogram is practical and brandable. Best for creating something that feels like a true standalone brand within the Triumph family.
