How the TC Works Here

The original logo uses TC in a chrome/silver circular bezel. This version drops the chrome circle for a shield frame and shifts the TC from silver to bronze — same script, completely different energy. The shield gives it an institutional, crest-like quality that says "managed program" without needing to spell it all out. The TC remains the hero, so anyone who knows the Triumph brand instantly recognizes it.

How the TC Works Here

This is the most natural fit for the TC — the original logo already uses a circle, so we're essentially modernizing the bezel. Strip the chrome/3D effect, make it a clean single-weight ring, and recolor the TC in teal. The italic Playfair "TC" script inside a geometric circle creates a nice tension between classic and modern. The small peak accent in the horizontal lockup adds the "growth" symbolism without overwhelming the TC mark.

How the TC Works Here

Cormorant Garamond gives the TC a slightly different serif character than the original — more editorial, less scripty — while keeping the italic elegance. The laurel wreath framing on the stacked version reinforces "Triumph" (victory/laurels) and the sage green positions growth and stability as the program's core promise. The gold thread accent is the through-line back to the parent brand. On the simplified versions, it's just TC in a clean circle — maximally simple, instantly recognizable.

Quick Comparison

All Three with TC — At a Glance

Heritage Elevated

Shield frame, bronze TC. Most connected to parent brand. Traditional, institutional feel. Best for: conservative advisor base that values continuity.

Modern Authority

Clean circle, teal TC. Most natural evolution of original logo shape. Tech-forward, data-driven energy. Best for: a platform that will be heavily dashboard/analytics focused.

Refined Distinction

Circle + laurel option, sage TC with gold thread. Strongest standalone identity while maintaining connection. Best for: building a sub-brand advisors identify with as its own thing.
